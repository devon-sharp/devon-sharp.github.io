Week 10 & 11: Machine Learning.

I used machine learning to conduct a regression analysis of GDP per capita on average percentage of population affected by natural disasters (droughts, floods & extreme weather events). I would hypothesise that countries with a lower GDP per capita are more prone to fall victim of extreme weather events. This is because areas such as Africa and Southeast Asia have lower incomes and often are effected by natural disasters/extreme weather.

(Colab)

As predicted, the regression suggests that there is a negative relationship between these two factors. However, the trend is not as strong as one might expect.

For my second machine learning application I tested whether gdp per capita and tax revenues (as a % of GDP) had a relationship. One would assume that countries with larger GDPs make more in tax revenues.

(Colab)

I selected 4 clusters since the World Bank assigns the world's economies to four income groups: low, lower-middle, upper-middle, and high-income countries. We observe that low-income countries have higher tax revenues as a % of GDP. This may be because their nominal tax revenue is not that large but, as a proportion of GDP, it is.
